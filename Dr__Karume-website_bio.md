# Our Founder

Dr. Michelle Karume is a Marriage and Family Therapist (MFT) clinician and professor, with specializations in Medical family therapy (MedFT), Program development and Evaluation, she is an author and a Leader in Systemic Family Therapy in Africa. Her core values are health and spirituality which both lend themselves to her philosophy that health is not the absence of disease but rather the inclusion of biological, psychological, and spiritual wellbeing. The Biopsychosocialspiritual framework is at the cornerstone of all her clinical and research work.

- Her research and leadership skills have lent themselves to developing MFT programs in Kenya, in the development of The Systemic Family Therapy Association-Kenya where she also serves as the Advisor on the board. Her current research is on "Collaborative Stress", a stress management model she recently coined in lieu of answering the current stress burden that many are grappling with.
- Her clinical interests have lent themselves to running a solo private practice for over 10 ten years. Sessions encompass: individual, couples, children, group therapy and family therapy. She also runs the annual Recro Grief Camp for grieving children.
- For corporate organizations she facilitates mental health discussions, and give trainings on the same.
- As an educator she has the privilege of teaching at the Undergraduate, Masters and Doctorate level.
- As an author-she finds ways of connecting complex theoretical practices and brings them to your family living room as a way of healing and enhancing family cohesion.

As a student of life Dr. Karume continues to immerse herself in knowledge; whereby her classroom is beyond the four walls of her clinical and research practices.
